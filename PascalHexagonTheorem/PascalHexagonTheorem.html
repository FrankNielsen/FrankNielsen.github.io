Pascal's hexagon theorem

Theorem: The intersection points of the lines passing through opposite edges of an hexagon inscribed on a conic are collinear.

This holds for any hexagon, possibly intersecting, the line passing through the 3 intersection points is called Pascal's line. The proof relies on projective geometry. Therefore there is a dual theorem called the Brianchon's theorem
