  • Some contributions to information geometry:
    • Bregman Voronoi diagrams, Discrete and Computational Geometry, 2010. → Bregman Voronoi diagrams can be built by clipping power diagrams, or by calculating the intersection of halfspaces, space of Bregman spheres, etc.
    • The Burbea-Rao and Bhattacharyya centroids, IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, 2011. → Statistical Bhattacharyya distances between exponential families yield Burbea-Rao parameter divergences. Centroid calculated using the Convex-ConCave Procedure (CCCP)
    • An information-geometric characterization of Chernoff information, IEEE Signal Processing Letters, 2013. → On the exponential family manifold, we characterize the best error exponent by calculating the intersection of a geodesic with a dual bisector. The Chernoff information (alpha-divergence for the optimal error exponent) amounts to a Bregman divergence.
    • On the Geometry of Mixtures of Prescribed Distributions, IEEE ICASSP 2018 → The Kullback-Leibler divergence between two mixture densities with prescribed components (w-mixtures) amount to a Bregman divergence for the negentropy Bregman generator. Report applications of this fact.
