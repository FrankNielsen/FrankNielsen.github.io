Information geometry

Information Geometry (IG) aims at unravelling geometric structures of families of families of probability distributions: The statistical models. Since geometric structures shape spaces, these structures can also be used in non-statistical contexts too.

Tutorials and surveys

  • The Many Faces of Information Geometry, AMS Notices, 2022.

    A gentle short introduction to information geometry in 8+1 pages

  • An Elementary Introduction to Information Geometry, Entropy, 2020.

    A self-contained introduction to classic information geometry with applications in about 70 pages

  • On Geodesic Triangles with Right Angles in a Dually Flat Space, book chapter in "Progress in Information Geometry", Springer, 2021.

    A self-contained introduction to dually flat spaces that we termed Bregman manifolds

  • Cramer-Rao Lower Bound and Information Geometry, Connected at Infinity II: On the work of Indian mathematicians (R. Bhatia and C.S. Rajan, Eds.), special volume of Texts and Readings In Mathematics (TRIM), Hindustan Book Agency, 2013

    A description of the pathbreaking paper of Calyampudi Radhakrishna Rao (1945): "Information and the accuracy attainable in the estimation of statistical parameters", 1945
